# LIMINAL — Sales & Marketing Strategy

> **"Zero-Trust Badge-Native Infrastructure"**

## Executive Summary

LIMINAL is a procedurally generated access card platform with zero users, zero revenue, and zero intention of changing either. This document outlines a comprehensive go-to-market strategy that will never go to market.

## Tagline Development

### Phase 1: Bureaucratic Authority

These taglines explored the tension between looking official and being absolutely nothing.

- "Your identity, but procedurally questionable"
- "Official-looking enough to confuse a security guard"
- "All badge, no authority"
- "Credentials not included"
- "Looks legit. Isn't."
- "The ID your HR department warned you about"

**Assessment:** Too honest. Marketing should never be honest.

### Phase 2: Existential / Philosophical

An attempt to make people question their own existence while looking at a fake card.

- "You are now a person who exists (citation needed)"
- "Proof of existence (pending review)"
- "Identity is a spectrum. This is the weird end."
- "Who are you? This card won't help."
- "Be someone. Specifically, someone with a lanyard."
- "Procedurally generated. Existentially questionable."

**Assessment:** Too introspective. Focus groups (imaginary) reported feeling "seen" and we can't have that.

### Phase 3: Deadpan Technical

Marketing copy that sounds like it was written by a backend engineer who just discovered feelings.

- "One seed. Infinite middle management."
- "Enterprise-grade make-believe"
- "Your entire identity, derived from a number"
- "4 billion people, none of them real"
- "Turning integers into impostor syndrome since 2024"
- "Math, but make it HR"

**Assessment:** Strong. The word "enterprise" tested well with people who have never worked at an enterprise.

### Phase 4: Corporate Dystopia

Lean into the vibe that this tool exists in a universe where HR has achieved sentience and badge access is a human right.

- "Badge in. Zone out."

- "Now boarding: floors that don't exist"
- "Security theatre, but make it generative art"
- "The lanyard-industrial complex"
- "Where every employee is a hash collision"

**Assessment:** Getting warmer. Needs more buzzwords.

### Phase 5: Self-Aware / Meta

For the audience that reads the fine print on their own fake ID card.

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Awareness (Months 1-3)

**Objective:** Make people aware that LIMINAL exists.

**Tactics:**
- Post on Twitter/X. Get 3 likes (two from bots, one from mum).
- Submit to Hacker News. Get one comment: "Why not just use LaTeX?"
- Share on Reddit r/generativeart. Immediately get asked "but can it run DOOM?"

**KPIs:**
- GitHub stars: Target 12 (stretch goal: 15)
- Unique visitors: Enough to crash a free-tier analytics dashboard
- Derek conversations: 0 (Derek hasn't been built yet and honestly that's fine)

### Phase 2: Engagement (Months 4-6)

**Objective:** Get people to actually use the thing.

**Tactics:**
- Add more sliders. Research shows engagement correlates directly with slider count.
- Add a pricing page with tiers that cost real money for software that's free. This is not a monetisation strategy. This is comedy.
- Create a "Share Your Card" feature so people can send links to cards that look vaguely like corporate IDs but contain fine print about elevator music being a bop.

**KPIs:**
- Average sliders tweaked per session: 23+
- Cards exported: Some
- Support tickets filed with Derek: 0 (Derek still doesn't exist)

### Phase 3: Monetisation (Never)

**Objective:** Do not monetise.

**Tactics:**
- Continue offering everything for free
- Maintain the pricing page purely as performance art
- If someone actually tries to pay, display a dialog that says "We appreciate your confidence in our fictional business model"

**KPIs:**
- Revenue: $0.00
- Revenue growth: 0% (consistent quarter over quarter)
- Burn rate: One developer's free time

All sales inquiries are handled by Derek, our enterprise sales chatbot who has enthusiasm but not answers. Derek's core competencies include:

- Responding to every question with "Great question!" followed by an unrelated product fact
- Scheduling demos that are just the user opening a URL
- Sending follow-up emails that contain only the word "Synergy?"

## Appendix A: The Pricing Page as Performance Art

The LIMINAL pricing page features four tiers:

1. **Pro** ($16.99/mo) — Paywalls features that are already free, including "Smart Export Pacing" (a rate limit) and "Premium 3-Second Render Delay" (artificial latency as a feature).

2. **Corporate** ($7/user/mo) — Includes a dedicated account manager named Derek. Derek is a chatbot. Derek has enthusiasm.

3. **Umbral** (█████/mo) — By invitation only. Features are redacted. The NDA is also redacted. "If you have to ask, you're not ready."

4. **Enterprise** (Let's Talk) — For organisations that need a receipt. Includes "white-glove onboarding" (we watch you open the URL) and a Quarterly Business Review (we show you a card).

The fine print at the bottom references the Fee Fee ($1.99/mo) and the Fee For The Fee Fee ($0.99/mo). Cancellation requires registered mail to our Zurich office, singed in triplicate — singed, not signed.

This is the entire monetisation strategy. It is also the entire joke.
